# Computational Thinking

## What You Will Learn

- What computational thinking is and why it matters
- Number theory fundamentals (primes, GCD, LCM, modular arithmetic)
- Digit manipulation techniques
- Programming basics and debugging strategies
- Time and space complexity analysis

## Why This Matters

Computational thinking is the foundation of all algorithmic problem-solving. Before you learn data structures or patterns, you need to think in steps, recognize patterns, and translate mathematical ideas into code. Every problem in this curriculum builds on these fundamentals.

## Prerequisites

None. This is the starting point.

## Estimated Time

2-3 weeks (1-2 hours daily)
